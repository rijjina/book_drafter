# QC Rubric

## Status Scale

- `PASS`: evidence is complete and no blocking issue remains.
- `CONDITIONAL PASS`: the structure is viable, but named non-blocking revisions remain.
- `FAIL`: a governing requirement, academic-integrity requirement, or central content requirement is not met.

## Automatic Blockers

- wrong or unconfirmed document classification;
- missing governing standard;
- missing course scope/evidence for a ตำรา or เอกสารคำสอน;
- missing disciplinary scope or author contribution for a หนังสือ;
- major factual error or unsupported central claim;
- invented citation, research result, course mapping, permission, or author experience;
- plagiarism, near-translation, duplicate publication, or unlicensed figure/table use;
- missing essential course topics for a teaching document;
- no coherent central concept or severe chapter fragmentation;
- work previously assessed under a prohibited category without demonstrable redevelopment;
- failure to meet a mandatory institutional component.

## Outline Gate

Check and record:

1. document type and target quality are explicit;
2. title, scope, readers, and central concept agree;
3. course/CLO mapping is complete where relevant;
4. chapters form a logical progression and do not duplicate one another;
5. every chapter has outcomes, evidence, author contribution, examples, and assessment plans appropriate to type;
6. high-risk claims and permission needs are identified early;
7. overall length and chapter balance are plausible;
8. front matter, references, appendices, glossary, and index are planned.

## Chapter Gate

Check and record:

1. alignment with the approved outline;
2. correctness, currency, completeness, and depth;
3. logical sequence and independent readability;
4. analysis/synthesis rather than compilation;
5. appropriate author viewpoint, experience, or research;
6. useful examples, cases, figures, tables, and exercises;
7. complete citations and permissions;
8. formal Thai and terminology consistency;
9. objective-content-assessment alignment where relevant;
10. summary and transition to the next chapter.

## Final Gate For ตำรา/หนังสือ

Evaluate:

- definition and required form;
- Level B baseline;
- additional Level A or A+ evidence when targeted;
- work-wide unity, chapter balance, and central concept;
- current and complete scholarship;
- author contribution and disciplinary value;
- front/back matter, bibliography, index, and navigation;
- publication, peer-review, dissemination, ISBN, and permission evidence as applicable;
- proofreading, design, and accessibility.

## Final Gate For Teaching Documents

Score the eight official-style criteria in `teaching-document-criteria.md`, show evidence for every score, and calculate the mean. Require `ดี` or higher unless a current local rule states otherwise.

For this skill's fixed `A-equivalent` production target for เอกสารคำสอน, additionally require:

- mean `3.26-4.00` (`ดีมาก`);
- no individual criterion below `3`;
- zero blockers;
- complete evidence of currency, depth, research/source breadth, relevant author experience, and analysis/synthesis comparable to Level A.

Label this result `A-equivalent` and state that it is an internal production target, not an official institutional grade.

## QC Report Rule

Use one primary rubric in the final decision. List secondary-manual findings in a separate advisory section so they do not silently change the governing score.
